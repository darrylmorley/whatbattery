# WhatBattery 1.0

The first stable release. WhatBattery reads your Mac's true battery health and the live power going in and out, and keeps the long-term record no other battery app does. It reads the same data Apple does, straight from IOKit and the SMC, with no rounding. Read-only: it never changes how your devices charge. macOS 14 and later, Apple Silicon.

## Free

- **True battery health** (real maximum capacity and cycle count, to one decimal) and **live power in and out**, in the menu bar and a main window.
- **Service condition** (Normal / Service Recommended / Service Battery), read from the system so it matches System Settings, alongside the full device and battery detail.
- **Bluetooth accessory levels** for a keyboard, mouse, trackpad, or AirPods (per bud and case).
- A bundled `whatbattery` command-line tool.

## Pro

- **Lifetime Analyzer:** a live history of charge, temperature, voltage, and power, with charts and min/avg/max over a selectable range.
- **Battery Health History:** a long-term, per-device record of monthly health and cycles, kept for years, with backup and restore.
- **Battery runway:** a forecast, not just today's number. The wear rate, the projected date your battery reaches the 80% service threshold, and a flag when it starts ageing faster than its own trend. No other battery app names a date.
- **Charging sessions:** every charge recorded, with how fast it ran, how hot it got, and a charger verdict ("58W of 96W") that tells you whether the charger kept up with what your Mac can take.
- **iPhone and iPad battery:** the health, cycle count, and live power of a connected device, read straight from your Mac over a cable or Wi-Fi, with nothing installed on the device.
- **Threshold notifications** for charge, temperature, and health, plus **accessory low-battery alerts**.
- **Reports and export:** a one-page battery report (PDF / print), and CSV / JSON export of your logged history.

Pro is a one-time purchase and works on up to two Macs.
